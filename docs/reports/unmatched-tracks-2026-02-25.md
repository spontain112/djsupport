## Matching Strategy Observations

### Common patterns in unmatched tracks

1. **Mastering/production artifacts in title** — Tracks with 'Master', 'MSTRD', '16Bit', '44khz', 'CALYX', 'Warm Audio Master', 'DIY MASTER' etc. in the title. These are pre-release or promo files where the filename was never cleaned up.

2. **Label/catalog noise** — Titles containing label names ('SELADOR', 'Moblack Records', 'Exploited Ghetto'), catalog numbers ('FB002', 'FB005'), or distribution site names ('DEEPDJ.ORG').

3. **File extensions in title** — Some titles end with '.mp3', '.aiff' — likely imported from filenames rather than proper metadata.

4. **Missing artist** — 41 tracks have no artist field at all, making search nearly impossible without title parsing.

5. **Complex remix attribution** — Titles like 'Track Name (Artist A & Artist B Remix) (Label Name)' where the real searchable title is buried in noise.

### Potential matching improvements

1. **Title cleaning** — Strip mastering artifacts (Master, MSTRD, 16Bit, 44khz, CALYX, DIY, Warm Audio, etc.) before searching
2. **Label stripping** — Remove known label names and catalog numbers from titles
3. **File extension removal** — Strip .mp3/.aiff/.wav/.flac from titles
4. **Artist extraction from title** — When artist is empty, try to parse 'Artist - Title' from the title field
5. **Parenthetical cleanup** — Remove nested parentheticals that aren't remix info (e.g., '(Torture the Artist Exclusive)', '(Phase Audio Premaster)')
